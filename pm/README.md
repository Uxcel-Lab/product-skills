# Product Management Skills

From framing the real problem to shipping specs, roadmaps, and metrics, and auditing all of it for rigor. **21 skills** for product management work, grouped by category. Each links to its `SKILL.md`.

---

## Types of skills

There are three kinds, and each one is used a little differently.

**Generative skills** (foundations, deliverables, processes) produce or shape work. Instead of applying every best practice at once, they *gate*: they establish context first, apply the always-true core, then surface the context-dependent choices with trade-offs and let you decide. Reach for these when you are creating a document or planning a process. Answer their questions rather than rushing past them, because that is where the rigor comes from.

**Evaluative skills** (audits) review existing work against a standard and hand back a prioritized, severity-rated list of issues, each with a concrete fix. Reach for these whenever you have something to check (a spec, a set of OKRs, a prioritized backlog) and want to know what is weak and what matters most. You can run several at once for a fuller picture.

**Orchestrators** (workflows) are the front doors. They compose the other two for you: review a product artifact holistically, or take a raw idea all the way to a build-ready plan. Reach for these when you would rather not pick individual skills yourself.

> **New here?** Try [`deliverables/product-spec`](./deliverables/product-spec/SKILL.md) first. Ask your assistant to *"write a PRD for [feature]"* and watch it anchor to a validated problem with measurable requirements instead of a wish list. To take a raw idea all the way to a build-ready plan, [`workflows/idea-to-plan`](./workflows/idea-to-plan/SKILL.md) chains the whole sequence.

## Foundations

The framing artifacts everything else builds on.

*Try: "Turn this feature request into a sharp, testable problem statement."*

| Skill | Purpose |
|---|---|
| [problem-statement](./foundations/problem-statement/SKILL.md) | Turn a vague prompt or request into a clear, evidence-grounded, testable statement of the real problem. |
| [personas-jtbd](./foundations/personas-jtbd/SKILL.md) | Research-based personas or a Jobs-to-Be-Done framing, and the choice between them. Never invented fiction. |
| [user-story](./foundations/user-story/SKILL.md) | Write user stories framed around who, what, and why, kept INVEST and testable, or critique existing ones. |

## Deliverables

The documents product managers ship.

*Try: "Write a PRD for [feature], anchored to a validated problem."*

| Skill | Purpose |
|---|---|
| [product-spec](./deliverables/product-spec/SKILL.md) | Write a PRD or spec anchored to a validated problem, with measurable requirements and surfaced risks. |
| [roadmap](./deliverables/roadmap/SKILL.md) | Outcome-oriented, theme-based, confidence-aware (Now-Next-Later) roadmaps tied to strategy. |
| [okrs-kpis](./deliverables/okrs-kpis/SKILL.md) | OKRs, KPIs, or a North Star Metric with outcome-based key results, baselines, targets, and guardrails. |
| [vision-strategy](./deliverables/vision-strategy/SKILL.md) | An aspirational destination plus the trade-off-driven choices to reach it, not a buzzword poster. |
| [competitive-analysis](./deliverables/competitive-analysis/SKILL.md) | A competitive or market analysis that yields a strategic edge, not a flat feature table. |
| [gtm-plan](./deliverables/gtm-plan/SKILL.md) | A stage-appropriate go-to-market system: customers, channels, pricing, timing, and partnerships. |

## Processes

How product managers work: discovery, prioritization, testing, analytics.

*Try: "Plan discovery for this idea before we commit to building it."*

| Skill | Purpose |
|---|---|
| [discovery](./processes/discovery/SKILL.md) | Plan continuous discovery to figure out what to build before building it, matching evidence to risk. |
| [prioritization](./processes/prioritization/SKILL.md) | Decide what to build first: the right framework, scoring that exposes trade-offs, sequencing, and saying no. |
| [assumption-testing](./processes/assumption-testing/SKILL.md) | Decompose an idea into load-bearing beliefs, then test the riskiest with the cheapest method. |
| [experimentation-ab](./processes/experimentation-ab/SKILL.md) | Design A/B tests with rigor: pre-registered hypothesis, power up front, guardrails, and a decision rule. |
| [analytics](./processes/analytics/SKILL.md) | Analysis that produces a decision, not a dashboard: actionable metrics, segmentation, and insight to action. |

## Audits _(evaluative)_

Review existing product management work against a standard and return a prioritized, severity-rated issue list with fixes.

*Try: "Audit these OKRs for vanity metrics and gameable targets."*

| Skill | Purpose |
|---|---|
| [spec-quality](./audits/spec-quality/SKILL.md) | Audit a spec or PRD for untestable requirements, happy-path-only flows, and missing scope and assumptions. |
| [prioritization-rigor](./audits/prioritization-rigor/SKILL.md) | Audit scored backlogs and roadmap orderings for false precision, strategy drift, and must-have inflation. |
| [okr-metric-validity](./audits/okr-metric-validity/SKILL.md) | Audit OKRs, KPIs, and metrics for vanity metrics, unfalsifiable KRs, outputs disguised as outcomes, and gameable targets. |
| [assumption-rigor](./audits/assumption-rigor/SKILL.md) | Audit how a team validated its assumptions: riskiest versus comfortable, method fidelity, and after-the-fact criteria. |
| [decision-quality](./audits/decision-quality/SKILL.md) | Audit a decision or its rationale for resulting, reversibility mismatch, bias, and missing pre-mortems. |
